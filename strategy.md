## The Problem

Most organizational dissatisfaction is latent. Workers cannot tell whether their concerns are shared. That uncertainty — not policy, law, or fear of management — is the primary suppressor of collective action.

The information asymmetry is structural: management has aggregate signals (surveys, exit interviews, turnover rates); individual workers have only their own experience. No trusted channel exists for workers to aggregate their own signal without exposure.

Result: even where majority dissatisfaction exists, individuals behave as minorities. Collective possibility remains invisible to the people who constitute it.

---

## The Core Innovation: Threshold Coordination

A worker does not simply express an opinion. They make a **conditional commitment**: *"I would take this action if N other verified coworkers do the same."* When the threshold is reached, all commitments activate simultaneously.

This eliminates the first-mover disadvantage. It converts isolated private sentiment into synchronized collective possibility. It preserves anonymity until — and optionally after — the threshold is met.

**The threshold mechanism is not a feature. It is the product.**

## Strategic Principles

| # | Principle | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Anonymity is load-bearing** | Anonymity is not a privacy feature — it is the precondition for participation. Any re-identification risk undermines the entire value proposition. |
| 2 | **Graduated participation** | Workers move through on-ramps. No feature should force a fully public or adversarial position before sufficient collective support exists. |
| 3 | **Threshold before action** | Nothing surfaces publicly or to management until a threshold is met. Partial disclosure is the most dangerous state for individual workers. |
| 4 | **Legibility without surveillance** | Organizations understand collective dynamics without visibility into individual behavior. Aggregate insight, not monitoring. |
| 5 | **Not a complaint platform** | Coordination infrastructure, not a vent channel. This framing matters for trust, adoption, and regulatory positioning. |
| 6 | **Each phase earns the next** | Later phases work only if earlier trust and coordination infrastructure is solid. No phase should be rushed to unlock revenue. |
| 7 | **Org-side revenue cannot compromise worker-side trust** | If a business model decision would erode worker trust, it is wrong regardless of revenue impact. |

## Platform Evolution

| Phase | Name | Core Value | Primary Actor |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Coordination & Visibility | Workers perceive themselves as a collective body | Workers |
| 2 | Organizational Health Intelligence | Orgs understand collective dynamics — with worker consent | People leaders + Workers |
| 3 | Democratic Workplace Infrastructure | Workers participate in governance, not just signaling | Workers + Org executives |
| 4 | Cooperative Transition Infrastructure | Worker ownership as natural extension of existing practices | Worker councils + Founders |

Phase 4 is only viable because the earlier phases built the trust, coordination capacity, and governance habits required for ownership transitions. This sequencing is not arbitrary — it reflects the actual prerequisites for cooperative governance.

## What This Platform Is Not

| Category | Why it's out of scope |
|---|---|
| Union organizing tool | Adversarial framing, legally complex, undermines MVP adoption |
| Anonymous complaint board | No coordination mechanism, no action pathway, creates adversarial dynamic |
| HR / management tool | Employer-side only; violates the trust architecture |
| Cooperative conversion tool (at MVP) | Requires trust and governance foundations that don't yet exist |
| Employee surveillance system | Structurally incompatible with the anonymity architecture |

---

## Competitive Positioning

| Category | Existing products | Why Wrkr is different |
|---|---|---|
| Engagement surveys | Culture Amp, Lattice, Glint | Employer tools. Workers don't own the data or framing. Responses are performative. |
| Anonymous feedback | Blind, Glassdoor | Public venting. No coordination mechanism. No threshold model. |
| Union organizing | Union-specific software | Politically loaded, adversarial framing, inappropriate at MVP. |
| Cooperative platforms | Various co-op tools | Start at the end state, skip the trust-building layers. |
| HR platforms | Workday, BambooHR | Employer-side only. No worker agency. |

**Moat:** Threshold coordination model + anonymous verification + graduated participation pathway. No existing product combines all three.

## North Star Metrics

| Phase | Primary metric | Secondary metric |

| 4 | Ownership transitions initiated | Employee ownership % achieved |
